Understanding Air Quality

Knowledge is the first step toward a healthier lifestyle. Learn how to interpret the numbers and protect yourself.

AQI Categories & Recommendations

0 - 50 (Good)

Air quality is satisfactory. You can enjoy outdoor activities freely. Great time for a morning run!

51 - 100 (Moderate)

Acceptable air quality. However, sensitive individuals may experience minor respiratory irritation.

101 - 200 (Poor)

Breathing discomfort to people with lungs, asthma and heart diseases. Wear a mask if outside for long periods.

201 - 300 (Very Poor)

Health alert: everyone may begin to experience health effects. Avoid prolonged outdoor exertion.

301 - 400 (Severe)

Respiratory effects even on healthy people. Serious health impact on those with existing diseases.

400+ (Hazardous)

Emergency condition. The entire population is more likely to be affected. Stay indoors with air purifiers.

Major Pollutants

PM2.5

Fine Particulate Matter

Microscopic particles (less than 2.5 microns) that can enter deep into the lungs and even the bloodstream. Often caused by vehicle exhaust and burning wood or coal.

PM10

Coarse Particulate Matter

Dust, pollen, and mold particles. While larger than PM2.5, they still cause irritation to the eyes, nose, and throat.

NO₂

Nitrogen Dioxide

Primarily gets into the air from the burning of fuel. High levels can cause inflammation of the airways and aggravate asthma.
